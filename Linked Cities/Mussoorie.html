An Insight into Mussoorie Tourism

Mussoorie, also known as Queen of the Hills, is among the most popular hill stations of the country. A Britisher, Captain Frederick Young, accompanied by an official named FJ Shore, had climbed up the hill from the Doon valley in 1827 and found this ridge offering great views and a salubrious climate. This visit laid the foundation for this grand hill station.

This magnificent hill station is among the top picks of all adventure seekers as it offers some of the best adventure sports to travelers. Places like Adventure Park Mussoorie offer activities such as biking tours, ziplining, hiking, and camping options. Other best adventure activities travelers like to be part of in the valleys of Mussoorie, Uttarakhand include trekking and mountain climbing.

Crammed with spectacular landscape, lush green forests, and trekking trails, Mussoorie is not only meant for nature lovers and adventurous souls, but it is also a place where religious people can also find bliss. Pilgrims can get their prayers answered in the most pious way by paying respects at the Shedup Choeppelling Temple, Nag Devta Temple and the Jwala Devi Temple which attracts religious seekers from all over India.

An ideal Mussoorie travel guide promises tourists royal visits to Camel’s Back Road and Lal Tibba for some jaw-dropping views from the magnificent peaks of the region. Here, wanderers get a chance to witness the elegance of one of the best Mussoorie destinations, the likes of which include the indomitable Kempty Falls and Jharipani Falls suggested primarily by every travel and tourism guide. With the help of informative tour guides crafted by experts, one can get up and close with historic sites such as the Gun Hill Point. In Mussoorie, parks like Company Garden and Municipal Garden put up for tourists the most colourful exhibitions of prismatic flower beds that flaunt seasonal hybridization. Among other places to visit here, one can also list down the famous SOHAM Heritage and Art Centre, which is an ideal spot for art seekers and can be visited on a day’s tour.
